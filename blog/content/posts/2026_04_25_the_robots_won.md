## the war is over
i can no longer take refuge in denialism.

two years ago - i would have told you that the impact of ai was vastly overstated. it's just a stochastic parrot; nothing but a markov chain in a trench coat.

last year - i would have said that it's a useful tool but no way is it replacing me.

now? idk man - even if my judgment, taste, experience, and knowledge are highly valuable to steer these things to writing production quality code - i just don't know if this is how i want to work anymore.

## and the robots won
i don't think i even really blame ai here. there's just a fundamental problem with the management class.

the capital class has us all by the fucking balls. and they are **squeezing** like a vice gripe.

i took a pay cut to join my current company. in retrospect, it was a dumb decision - but i did so because the stress of my previous job just wasn't worth it anymore. i joined my current company pretty explicitly for the work-life balance and was pleasantly surprised to find myself working on an extremely high quality and interesting codebase.

but now? same as everywhere else - management here is engorged in the ai feeding frenzy. very rapidly our work-life balance has degraded. despite this company having doubled revenue since i joined - without nearly any budging in headcount. yet that still isn't enough.

in an all hands they announced that they believe we can achieve (and by that implication - wants to see) a doubling/tripling/quadrupling/and more of productivity. this isn't just a fever dream - this is a thinly veiled threat.

and i can tell my coworkers feel threatened by it too. not just because they've directly told me so - but because everybody feels the need to work at 150% effort all day every day with these machines. it's impossible to keep up.

meanwhile they've been slowly scaling back our benefits and culture that once made this place so unique and special. really just boiling us frogs. 

we used to have a ton of floating holidays in lieu of official holidays off. that flexibility was clutch for taking more trips throughout the year. we used to have an annual company-wide retreat. then it was every year and a half (once) and now it's just gone. we used to have a week-long hackathon in engineering to build our dreams. now? they don't want to see us engineers empowered to build our dream features in our beloved product - they just want us building more ai tooling. as if that's not what we do all day every day. ugh!

so what's the point anymore? ai has just sucked the fun out of the room - and im getting paid less!

## claude rules everything around me
my team works on some pretty complex and high quality code. i found it to be an extremely satisfying work environment. i could take my time to deeply understand the problem space, and come back with a major refactor that unlocked a slew of new functionality while simplifying things and improving performance. that was so damn satisfying. i really felt like i had achieved a rare sense of purpose from work. i felt so much pride in the career i had managed to build for myself.

but now? 

>just go with what claude wrote, bro. claude can just refactor it later, bro. just ship a little faster, bro. you dont even need to look at the code anymore, bro.
{.styled-quote}

all i do is review llm code. code claude wrote for me. code claude wrote for my teammates. code claude wrote for people that wish our service did some crazy thing for them. im just drowning in pull requests and its becoming harder to push back on anything because "claude can just refactor it later". ugh.

i don't think i was ever the worlds best engineer. but in the "old" times i was pretty decent! maybe not the fastest, but definitely fairly fast. and i felt like my contributions were decently high quality and raised the bar on the team and the company. i felt like a useful mentor to my more junior peers.

but now?

im just back to being a code monkey again. just push what claude writes straight to prod - who cares anymore. 

i worked my ass off for damn near a decade both to self-teach my way into software engineering and then to really accelerate my growth in this career. what a fucking defeat.

## rumors of a resistance
i think staying in my current role doesn't really make sense anymore.

if im not going to get the work-life balance that i want - then i might as well go back to just maximizing dollars for my labor.

on the other hand - i feel like now is the time to try to build something. maybe the only way out of this rat race is to take my actually valuable skillset and just build for myself.

i have a product vision for something i want. and its something i know others want too. it's within my grasp to build. yes it will take a whole lot of work - but maybe i should just go for it and try to free myself from the yoke. might as well die trying if this status quo is the alternative.
